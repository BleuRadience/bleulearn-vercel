4-Point Scale for Metacognition Responses

ScoreCriteriaStudent Example
4Excellent: Explains reasoning with precise math vocabulary"I knew to divide because the problem said 'share equally among 6 friends.' The total was divided into 6 equal groups, so I wrote 24 ÷ 6 = 4."
3Good: Explains reasoning but missing key math vocabulary"I divided because it said share among 6 people."
2Basic: Vague explanation or "I just knew""I just knew to divide." or "It seemed right."
1Incomplete: Blank, off-topic, or illegible"I like pizza." or [blank]
📝 TEACHER NOTES:
  • Enter scores weekly as formative assessment
  • If most students score 2 or below -- reteach metacognition strategies
  • Use the poster sentence stems for students who are struggling
  • Never grade as punitive -- this is reflective practice

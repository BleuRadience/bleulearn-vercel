Strand 25: BleuField

K-12 | Quarterly documented field experiences connecting classroom primary-source work to physical places, archives, and communities. Every destination connects to a specific documented learning objective — this is not a generic field trip program.

The BleuLearn Standard Applied to Field Learning

Every BleuField destination is chosen because it holds, displays, or is itself a documented primary source connected to a specific volume or strand already being taught that quarter. A scholar does not visit a place to "have an experience" disconnected from the curriculum — they visit because the place IS a document, and they apply the four-question framework to it the same way they would to a paper source.

Grade-Band Destination Map (Virginia-Anchored, Free or Low-Cost Where Possible)

K-2: The Documented Local Community

  • Destination: The Pamunkey Indian Museum and Cultural Center (Pamunkey Reservation, King William County, VA) — connects to Indigenous Peoples Studies, Vol.2.
  • Destination: A local public library's archive room or local history room (free, most Virginia county libraries have one) — connects to Primary Source Literacy foundations.
  • Documented learning objective: Scholars apply a simplified WHO/WHEN/WHAT/WHY to one object or document encountered, with drawing support.

3-5: Virginia's Documented Indigenous and Colonial Record

  • Destination: The Library of Virginia (Richmond, free admission) — connects to BleuHistory Vol.2/3 and the Pamunkey Treaty of 1646.
  • Destination: Jamestown Settlement and Historic Jamestowne (paid admission; connects to Vol.2/4, taught critically against the Doctrine of Discovery content already in the curriculum, not as celebration).
  • Documented learning objective: Scholars locate and document one primary source connected to a volume already studied, citing the exact archive or exhibit case.

6-8: Documented Sites of Resistance and Erasure

  • Destination: The Equal Justice Initiative's Legacy Museum and National Memorial for Peace and Justice (Montgomery, AL — a multi-day trip requiring fundraising; connects directly to Vol.8-11 and the racial wealth gap unit in Financial Architecture).
  • Destination (lower-cost alternative): The Library of Virginia's documented exhibits on Reconstruction and Jim Crow, or a Richmond-area Civil Rights driving tour using free self-guided materials from the Virginia Department of Historic Resources.
  • Documented learning objective: Scholars connect a physical site to a specific primary source already studied and write a documented reflection linking the two.

9-12: Capstone-Connected Archive Access

  • Destination: Site visits are individualized to each scholar's Capstone research question — for example, a scholar researching the Tulsa Race Massacre may need access to a specific archive's reading room, which may require a documented research trip rather than a class-wide destination.
  • Destination: HBCU campus visits, which double as College and Career Readiness strand activities (see /college-career/).
  • Documented learning objective: Scholars document new primary sources located on-site directly into their Capstone bibliography with full BleuLearn citation.

⚠ Honest Note on What This Strand Still Needs

This destination map is real and specific, but it has NOT been operationally confirmed: no transportation budget, chaperone ratios, parental consent process, or institutional partnership agreements exist yet for any of these sites. That operational layer belongs with the school's administrative team, not a curriculum document, and should not be assumed complete just because the destinations are named here.

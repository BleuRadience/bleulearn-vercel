DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades K-12

Documented Greetings and the Language of the African Diaspora

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to produce a greeting and response in all five BleuLearn languages and name the African connection for at least three.

Free Resources (No Textbook)

Duolingo (free tier); TV5Monde (tv5monde.com, French); Mango Languages via public library card (Swahili, Spanish)

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • greeting: a culturally specific way of acknowledging another person
  • diaspora: dispersal of a people from their homeland, often through forced or voluntary migration

Instruction — Steps

  1. Post a map of all five language regions.
  2. Model each greeting twice with audio; class repeats 3x; partner practice.
  3. Teach the documented African connection for Arabic, French, Swahili, and Yoruba specifically.
  4. Greeting circuit: scholars rotate the room, switching languages every 45 seconds.

Discourse

Turn-and-Talk: which greeting do you think you'll remember tomorrow, and why?

BleuLearn Pause

"Document one thing you learned about where each language comes from."

Exit Ticket

"Say hello and thank you in two of the five languages. Document which two and why you chose them."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: focus on two languages with visual phonetic cards.
  • Grade-Level: all five languages, no visual support.
  • Gifted: research what Indigenous language was displaced where Spanish is now dominant, using native-land.ca.

Archive Assignment

At home (optional, no internet required if unavailable): 5 minutes on Duolingo in one language; document what you practiced.

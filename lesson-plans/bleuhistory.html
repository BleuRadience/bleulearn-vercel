DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades 5-12

The Kingdom of Kush and Egyptian Continuity (Vol.1)

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to analyze Piye's Victory Stele (c.728 BCE) as a primary source documenting Kushite pharaonic rule of Egypt, applying the four-question framework with at least one documented archive citation.

Free Resources (No Textbook)

Victory Stele of Piye — metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/544354; full translation at ucl.ac.uk/museums-static/digitalegypt/kushites/piye.html

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • sovereignty: documented political authority over a territory
  • stele: a carved stone slab documenting an event
  • Kush: ancient African kingdom south of Egypt, also documented as Nubia

Instruction — Steps

  1. Pre-teach vocabulary with Frayer Models before showing the document.
  2. Post the Stele image. 90 seconds silent observation, then Turn-and-Talk: what do you notice?
  3. Teacher reads the Document Box aloud. Scholars follow without annotating.
  4. Silent re-read. Scholars underline one claim that surprises them.
  5. Apply the four questions in pairs.

Discourse

Socratic Seminar (Grade 6) or Structured Academic Controversy (Grade 5): Is Kushite pharaonic rule of Egypt documented evidence that Egyptian civilization was African?

BleuLearn Pause

"What does the Victory Stele document that most textbooks leave out of their discussion of ancient Egypt?"

Exit Ticket

"Using the four-question framework, write one documented sentence each for WHO, WHEN, WHAT, and WHY about the Victory Stele of Piye."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: partially completed four-question template with sentence starters; small-group read-aloud first.
  • Grade-Level: full Document Box independently; citation documented independently.
  • Gifted: access the full UCL translation; find one claim not in the excerpt; bring it to Seminar.

Archive Assignment

Search the Met's collection (metmuseum.org) for 'Kushite' or 'Nubian pharaoh.' Find a different object. Apply the four questions and write a complete BleuLearn citation.

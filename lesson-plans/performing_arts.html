DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades K-12

The BleuLearn Verse Standard — Setting Mansa Musa's History to Rhythm

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to co-create an original academic verse documenting three primary-source facts, performed in a call-and-response structure.

Free Resources (No Textbook)

Documented call-and-response structures from West African oral tradition (cite specific sources studied in BleuHistory Vol.1)

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • call-and-response: a participatory musical structure where a leader's phrase is answered by a group
  • griot: a West African oral historian whose tradition this practice draws from

Instruction — Steps

  1. Review the day's BleuHistory content (Mansa Musa) and identify 3-5 documented facts as a class.
  2. Choose a rhythm collaboratively — clapping pattern or call-and-response.
  3. Set the facts to the rhythm together; teacher facilitates, scholars create.
  4. Perform the finished verse as a class.

Discourse

Partner Discussion: which fact was hardest to fit into rhythm, and why?

BleuLearn Pause

"Did setting this fact to rhythm change how well you remember it? Document your honest answer."

Exit Ticket

"Perform or write your verse line documenting one fact from today's history lesson."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: simplified rhythm pattern, fewer facts.
  • Grade-Level: full collaborative verse creation.
  • Gifted: add a second verse connecting to a different BleuHistory volume.

Archive Assignment

Find one additional documented fact about Mansa Musa from a primary source and write a verse line for it.

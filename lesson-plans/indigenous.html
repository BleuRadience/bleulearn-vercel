DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades K-12

The Pamunkey Nation: Sovereignty Before and After Contact

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to document the Pamunkey Nation's pre-contact governance and contemporary federal recognition status using primary and documented secondary sources.

Free Resources (No Textbook)

native-land.ca; pamunkey.org (official tribal documentation); Library of Virginia (virginiamemory.com)

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • sovereignty: documented political authority over a territory
  • federal recognition: formal U.S. government acknowledgment of a tribal nation's sovereign status

Instruction — Steps

  1. Post the native-land.ca map of the school's region. Identify the documented nation.
  2. Read primary/secondary sources on Powhatan Confederacy governance, 1607 context.
  3. Document the 1677 Treaty of Middle Plantation and what it established.
  4. Research and document the 2015 federal recognition and what changed.

Discourse

Socratic Seminar: what does federal recognition in 2015 — 408 years after first contact — document about the U.S. government's relationship to Indigenous sovereignty?

BleuLearn Pause

"What surprised you about the gap between 1607 and 2015?"

Exit Ticket

"Name the Pamunkey Nation's documented current federal status and one historical treaty governing their territory."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: guided worksheet with key dates provided.
  • Grade-Level: independent timeline construction.
  • Gifted: compare Pamunkey federal recognition timeline to one other Virginia tribe's recognition status.

Archive Assignment

Access pamunkey.org's official documentation and find one detail about contemporary tribal governance not covered in this lesson.

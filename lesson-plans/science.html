DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades K-12

Osmosis in Potato Cells Across Salt Concentrations

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to design and document an osmosis investigation using CER (Claim, Evidence, Reasoning).

Free Resources (No Textbook)

Khan Academy osmosis explainer (khanacademy.org, free); household materials (potatoes, salt, water, scale)

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • osmosis: water movement across a membrane toward higher solute concentration
  • isotonic: the point where concentration inside and outside a cell is equal

Instruction — Steps

  1. Background: read the Khan Academy explainer, cite it.
  2. Hypothesis: above 1% salt, potato mass decreases — name the mechanism.
  3. Procedure: cut uniform cylinders, weigh, soak in 5 concentrations, reweigh after 24 hours.
  4. Data and CER: claim, evidence (specific numbers), reasoning (mechanism, not just description).

Discourse

Socratic Seminar: What does the isotonic data point reveal about the potato cell's natural salinity?

BleuLearn Pause

"What was your isotonic point, and how do you know?"

Exit Ticket

"Explain in one documented sentence why mass decreased above the isotonic concentration."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: pre-measured concentrations provided; guided data table.
  • Grade-Level: full independent procedure and CER.
  • Gifted: test a second variable (temperature) and document its interaction with concentration.

Archive Assignment

Find a peer-reviewed osmosis study (search Google Scholar for 'potato osmosis isotonic concentration') and compare your isotonic point to the documented literature value.

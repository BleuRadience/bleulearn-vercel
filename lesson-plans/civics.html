DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades 5-12

Tracking the John Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to trace a real bill's documented status and connect it to the historical voting rights timeline since 1870.

Free Resources (No Textbook)

congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/4; Oyez (oyez.org) for Shelby County v. Holder

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • preclearance: required federal approval before certain states change voting laws
  • cloture: the vote required to end debate and force a final Senate vote

Instruction — Steps

  1. Post the bill number and ask scholars to find its current status on congress.gov.
  2. Trace the documented history: 15th Amendment (1870) to Voting Rights Act (1965) to Shelby County (2013).
  3. Document the timeline of state law changes immediately after Shelby County.
  4. Build documented arguments for and against restoring preclearance.

Discourse

Socratic Seminar: does the speed of post-Shelby County changes prove preclearance was necessary?

BleuLearn Pause

"What single piece of evidence most strengthens the position you don't personally hold?"

Exit Ticket

"State the bill's current status with a congress.gov citation."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: guided timeline with key dates provided.
  • Grade-Level: independent timeline and position paper.
  • Gifted: research one additional documented case affecting voting rights since Shelby County.

Archive Assignment

Find your own state's current voter ID law and document whether it changed within 2 years of Shelby County.

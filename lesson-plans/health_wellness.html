DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades K-12

Documenting Sleep, Movement, and Mood Patterns

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to record a week of documented wellness data and identify one real pattern connecting two metrics.

Free Resources (No Textbook)

American Academy of Sleep Medicine guidelines (aasm.org, free); 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (988lifeline.org)

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • pattern: a documented relationship between two tracked variables over time
  • wellness: documented physical, mental, and social health taken together, not just absence of illness

Instruction — Steps

  1. Introduce the BleuLearn Wellness Tracker worksheet.
  2. Model one day of honest documentation as a class example.
  3. Scholars track independently for the week (or review a provided sample week if just introducing the tool).
  4. Analyze: does a low-sleep day correlate with a low-energy or low-mood day in the data?

Discourse

Turn-and-Talk: what's one pattern in your own data you didn't expect?

BleuLearn Pause

"What is one specific, concrete plan — not a vague intention — based on this week's pattern?"

Exit Ticket

"Name one pattern from your documented week and the specific plan it leads to."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: simplified tracker with fewer categories.
  • Grade-Level: full tracker, independent pattern analysis.
  • Gifted: graph the week's data and calculate a correlation, not just an observed pattern.

Archive Assignment

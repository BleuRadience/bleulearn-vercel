DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades K-12

The Documented Racial Wealth Gap — Cause and Strategy

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to explain at least three documented historical causes of the racial wealth gap and identify one generational wealth strategy with a real calculation.

Free Resources (No Textbook)

Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances (federalreserve.gov/publications/files/scf23.pdf); Mapping Inequality redlining maps (dsl.richmond.edu/panorama/redlining)

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • wealth: assets minus liabilities, not income
  • redlining: 1934-1968 federal policy denying mortgages in neighborhoods coded 'hazardous'
  • compound interest: growth of money over time at a percentage rate

Instruction — Steps

  1. Post '$171,000' and '$17,000' with no labels. Reveal: Federal Reserve median wealth figures by race.
  2. Cause 1 — slavery: 246 years of unpaid labor, cite Freedmen's Bureau records.
  3. Cause 2 — redlining: find your own neighborhood on the 1938 HOLC map at dsl.richmond.edu.
  4. Cause 3 — GI Bill exclusion: cite the NAACP's documented report.
  5. Strategy: calculate $100/month at 7% over 30 years ≈ $113,000.

Discourse

Socratic Seminar: which documented strategy is most likely to close a 10:1 ratio within one generation?

BleuLearn Pause

"Which cause was most significant to you, and what is the evidence for your choice?"

Exit Ticket

"Draw a comparison bar model of the wealth gap using Federal Reserve figures and name one historical cause."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: pre-drawn bar model template; guided cause selection.
  • Grade-Level: independent bar model and cause documentation.
  • Gifted: check whether the gap persists at equal income levels using Fed data by income quintile.

Archive Assignment

Access the Equal Justice Initiative's Lynching in America report (eji.org) and document one finding connecting racial terrorism to wealth destruction.

DOCUMENTED LESSON PLAN — Grades K-12

Mansa Musa and the Documented Wealth of Mali

Documented Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, scholars will be able to analyze Al-Umari's account of Mansa Musa's 1324 hajj as primary source evidence of Mali's documented wealth and global influence.

Free Resources (No Textbook)

Al-Umari's account (c.1337) excerpted in multiple academic translations; Catalan Atlas (1375) — bnf.fr

Vocabulary (Frayer Model Pre-Teach)

  • hajj: the Islamic pilgrimage to Mecca
  • griot: a West African oral historian and primary-source keeper

Instruction — Steps

  1. Post the Catalan Atlas image of Mansa Musa holding gold.
  2. Read Al-Umari's account: gold prices in Cairo fell for 12 years after Musa's visit.
  3. Apply the four questions to the document.
  4. Connect to Financial Architecture: comparison bar model of Musa's documented gold vs. contemporary European rulers.

Discourse

Socratic Seminar: what does a foreign price-collapse documented 700 years later prove about Mali's economic scale?

BleuLearn Pause

"What documented evidence most convinces you of Mali's wealth — the image or the economic data?"

Exit Ticket

"Cite one piece of documented evidence for Mali's 14th-century wealth, with its archive source."

Differentiation

  • Bridge: image-first entry point with guided questions.
  • Grade-Level: full document analysis independently.
  • Gifted: research what the Cairo gold-price collapse meant for the broader Mediterranean economy.

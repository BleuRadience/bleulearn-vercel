WS1 ANSWER KEY

📜 Primary Source Analysis

All Strands  |  Grades 3-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: Columbus's guanin diary entry, Oct-Nov 1492. Raccolta di documenti e studi, Vol.1 (1892-1894). archive.org

✅ Model Answer

WHO: Christopher Columbus, Italian-born explorer commissioned by the Spanish Crown, writing his personal daily log. He had a documented financial interest -- his contract with the Spanish Crown gave him 10% of all profits from discovered trade.

WHEN: October-November 1492, during first documented European contact with Caribbean peoples. Columbus was under pressure to demonstrate the voyage's value to Ferdinand and Isabella.

WHAT: The diary documents Columbus observing guanin (a gold-silver-copper alloy) worn by Taino people, noting it resembles what he saw in West Africa. He sends samples to Spain for assay.

WHY: This is documented primary source evidence for pre-Columbian contact between African peoples and the Americas. The assay confirmed West African production ratios -- this document is the foundation of the Vol.3 argument for African presence before Columbus.

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • All four questions answered in complete sentences.
  • WHO includes documented role AND documented interest (bias disclosure), not just a name.
  • WHAT quotes or closely paraphrases the document -- never substitutes interpretation for what it says.
  • Archive citation complete: Creator, Title, Date, Archive, URL.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • Answering WHO with only a name, no role or documented interest.
  • Answering WHAT with interpretation rather than what the document says.
  • Omitting the archive URL -- a citation without a URL cannot be verified by anyone.

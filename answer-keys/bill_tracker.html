WS22 ANSWER KEY

⚖️ Voting Rights Bill Tracker

Civics / BleuHistory  |  Grades 6-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: The John Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act and its documented historical context.

✅ Model Answer

Documented history: 15th Amendment (1870) guarantees the vote; poll taxes and literacy tests suppress it until the 1965 Voting Rights Act; Shelby County v. Holder (2013) strikes preclearance.

Evidence for urgency: Texas announced new voter ID law changes within 24 hours of the Shelby County ruling.

Documented position: The timeline itself -- changes within hours, not years -- is the strongest evidence preclearance was doing real work.

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • History traced back to the 15th Amendment, not starting at 1965.
  • Shelby County v. Holder cited with an oyez.org URL.
  • Position argued from documented timeline evidence, not opinion.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • History starting only at the Voting Rights Act, skipping the 15th Amendment.
  • Position stated with no supporting timeline or evidence.

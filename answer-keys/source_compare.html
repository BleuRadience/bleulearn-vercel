🔍 Primary Source Comparison

BleuHistory  |  Grades 6-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: (A) Columbus's guanin diary, 1492. (B) Da Gama's account of trading guanin in West Africa, 1498 (Roteiro da Primeira Viagem).

✅ Model Answer

Document A: Columbus observes guanin worn by Taino people in 1492, notes resemblance to West African items.

Document B: Da Gama confirms Portuguese traders exchanging guanin at West African ports in 1498.

Where they agree: Both confirm guanin was a recognized West African manufactured trade item.

Where they differ: Columbus observes it in the Americas six years before Da Gama documents direct West African trade of it -- raising the question of how it arrived before 1492.

Most useful source: Da Gama's account, because it directly corroborates that guanin was definitively West African, strengthening Columbus's identification.

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • Both documents identified with archive locations.
  • Agreement and difference sections cite specific language, not impressions.
  • Usefulness judgment is tied to evidential value, not preference.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • Comparing documents without citing either one.
  • Stating agreement/difference without quoting specific content.
  • Judging usefulness by personal interest rather than evidence.

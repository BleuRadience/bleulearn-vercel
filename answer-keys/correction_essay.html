WS7 ANSWER KEY

✍️ Documented Correction Essay

BleuHistory  |  Grades 8-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: Standard textbook claim: 'The Civil War had multiple equal causes -- economic differences, states' rights, and slavery.'

✅ Model Answer

Specific error: Presenting slavery as one of several co-equal causes rather than the cause the Confederate founders themselves named as primary.

Correcting source: Georgia's Declaration of Secession (1861, avalon.law.yale.edu): 'Our position is thoroughly identified with the institution of slavery.' Mississippi's declaration uses nearly identical language.

Documented correction: Every state secession declaration names slavery as the primary cause in the founders' own words. The 'multiple causes' framing traces to Mildred Lewis Rutherford's 1919 UDC 'Measuring Rod,' which instructed schools to reject textbooks naming slavery as primary cause.

Why this matters: 'Multiple causes' allows uncertainty about a question the founders answered in writing. Documented uncertainty serves documented power.

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • Source being corrected is specifically named.
  • Correction uses a primary source, not another secondary source.
  • Essay quotes the primary source's actual language with citation.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • Vague source identification ('a textbook I read').
  • Using a website or another textbook as the 'correcting' source instead of a primary source.
  • Essay that never quotes the primary source directly.

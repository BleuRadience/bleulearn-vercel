📁 Archive Assignment Guide

All Strands  |  Grades 3-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: National Archives (archives.gov). Search: 'Freedmen's Bureau records' or 'Dawes Rolls'.

✅ Model Answer

Archive: National Archives, Freedmen's Bureau Records, 1865-1872.

Document found: 'Letter from Freedmen's Bureau Agent, Raleigh, NC, documenting contract-violation complaints from formerly enslaved persons, 1866.' Archives Identifier: 300398.

WHO: A federal field agent documenting complaints. The Bureau had a documented role as intermediary between formerly enslaved workers and former enslavers.

WHEN: 1866, one year after the Civil War -- the earliest phase of Reconstruction.

WHAT: Documents plantation owners violating signed labor contracts: withheld wages, reduced rations, torn-up agreements.

WHY: Evidence that the transition from slavery to freedom was actively resisted by the former slaveholding class -- part of the documented history feeding today's racial wealth gap.

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • Archive name and URL specified, not just 'an archive.'
  • Document identified specifically enough that anyone else could find it.
  • All four questions answered using the actual document.
  • Citation includes Record Group number where applicable.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • Listing a general archive without naming a specific document.
  • WHAT answered with interpretation instead of content.
  • Omitting the catalog ID from the citation.

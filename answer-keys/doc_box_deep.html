📚 Document Box Deep Dive

BleuHistory  |  Grades 7-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: Alfonso I of Kongo, Letter to King João III of Portugal, 1526. Arquivo Nacional Torre do Tombo, Lisbon.

✅ Model Answer

Excerpt says: Alfonso I documents Portuguese traders illegally enslaving free Kongolese citizens, including royal family members, and requests Portugal stop it.

Full document adds: Alfonso operates within a documented diplomatic-legal framework, appeals to shared Christian values, names specific victims, and is enforcing existing Kongo-Portugal trading agreements -- not demanding slavery's universal end.

How this changes understanding: The excerpt shows outrage; the full letter shows a sovereign king's documented legal sophistication and specific grievances.

Documented significance: Evidence that African rulers formally protested the transatlantic slave trade within its first decade -- challenging the narrative that African rulers passively enabled it.

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • Full document accessed at the listed archive, not just the excerpt.
  • Specific content identified as new in the full document, not vague.
  • Argument connects directly to documented evidence.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • Summarizing the excerpt without accessing the full document.
  • 'It gave more detail' without naming what detail.
  • Argument based on feeling rather than evidence.

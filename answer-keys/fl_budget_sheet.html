WS13 ANSWER KEY

💰 Budget Sheet

Financial Architecture  |  Grades 3-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: Scenario: family of 4, South Chesterfield VA, documented monthly income $3,200.

✅ Model Answer

Savings (pay yourself first): $640 (20%, per the 50/30/20 framework).

Housing: $1,100 (documented average 2BR rent, area data). Food: $500 (USDA moderate plan, family of 4).

Total expenses: $2,990. Net: $3,200 − $2,990 = $210/month.

Reflection: A $1,000 emergency would erase nearly 5 months of discretionary income -- this is why an emergency fund is documented as essential before any discretionary spending.

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • Every figure has a documented source, not an invented number.
  • Savings calculated first, not last.
  • Reflection names a specific financial risk revealed by the numbers.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • Inventing numbers with no source.
  • Forgetting to budget savings as an expense category.

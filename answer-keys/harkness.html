WS2 ANSWER KEY

💬 Harkness Discussion Preparation

BleuHistory  |  Grades 7-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: Confederate States of Georgia, Declaration of Secession, Jan 29, 1861. avalon.law.yale.edu/19th_century/csa_geosec.asp

✅ Model Answer

Documented position: Confederate secession was motivated primarily by the documented intention to preserve and expand slavery, as the Confederate founders stated in their own declarations.

Primary source: Georgia's Declaration (1861): 'Our position is thoroughly identified with the institution of slavery -- the greatest material interest of the world.'

Strongest counterargument to expect: 'The Civil War was about states' rights, not slavery.'

Documented response: The founders named which specific right: 'a blow at slavery.' Mississippi's declaration states the identical phrase. The states'-rights framing does not appear in the founders' own documents.

Documented question for the circle: 'If the founders believed they fought for states' rights generally, why does every Confederate secession declaration explicitly name slavery as the reason?'

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • Position stated in one clear sentence before elaboration.
  • Primary source cited with archive URL.
  • Counterargument is the strongest real version, not a strawman.
  • Response to counterargument uses the primary source, not opinion.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • Stating a position without citing the primary source.
  • Treating the counterargument as obviously wrong instead of engaging it documentarily.
  • Citing a textbook or secondary source instead of a primary source.

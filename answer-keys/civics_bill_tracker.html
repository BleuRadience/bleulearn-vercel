WS16 ANSWER KEY

🏛️ Bill Tracker

Civics  |  Grades 5-12

📄 Document used in this model answer: John Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act, H.R.4, 117th Congress. congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/4

✅ Model Answer

What it proposes: Restores the Voting Rights Act preclearance requirement struck down in Shelby County v. Holder (2013, oyez.org).

Status: Passed House. Died in Senate (cloture failed 50-49, Jan 2022).

Documented position: Preclearance should be restored because documented state law changes began within hours of the Shelby County decision -- that timeline is evidence of cause and effect, not coincidence.

✔ Must Be Present for Full Credit

  • Bill cited with a congress.gov URL.
  • Both support and opposition documented fairly, not strawmanned.
  • Personal position is a documented argument, not just an opinion.

⚠️ Documented Common Errors for This Specific Worksheet

  • Bill described with no citation.
  • Opposing position presented as obviously wrong.

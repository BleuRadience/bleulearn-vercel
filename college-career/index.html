Strand 24: College and Career Readiness

Grades 9-12 | Distinct from the Capstone. The Capstone is an original research requirement. This strand is the documented, practical preparation for what comes after graduation — applications, financial aid, and career pathways, including paths that are not college.

Why This Is Documented as a Separate Strand from the Capstone

The Capstone (see BleuLearn_Capstone_Languages_Community_TeacherEval.docx) proves a scholar can conduct original documented research. It does not teach a scholar how to fill out the FAFSA, write a college application essay, compare financial aid packages, or evaluate a skilled-trade apprenticeship against a four-year degree. Both are required. Conflating them was an earlier gap in this curriculum's documentation; they are separated here.

Grade-by-Grade Scope

Grade 9-10: Documented Self-Assessment and Exploration

  • Scholars complete a documented interest and strengths inventory (free tool: O*NET Interest Profiler, mynextmove.org — no cost, federally maintained).
  • Scholars research three documented career pathways connected to their interests, including at minimum one pathway that does not require a four-year degree, using the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Outlook Handbook (bls.gov/ooh, free).
  • Scholars begin a documented activity and achievement log (the foundation of the eventual resume and application materials).

Grade 10-11: HBCU and Institutional Research

  • Scholars document and compare at minimum five institutions, with at least three being HBCUs, using each institution's published Common Data Set (publicly available on most institutional websites, free) for admission rate, cost, and outcomes data — not marketing materials.
  • Where feasible, the school organizes documented HBCU campus visits or virtual information sessions; if not feasible in a given year, scholars complete a documented virtual tour and submit written notes using the four-question framework applied to the institution's own published data.
  • Scholars research documented trade and apprenticeship pathways (Virginia Department of Labor and Industry apprenticeship database, doli.virginia.gov, free) with the same rigor applied to four-year institutions.

Grade 11-12: Application Mechanics

  • Scholars complete a documented FAFSA practice walkthrough using the U.S. Department of Education's official free FAFSA resources (studentaid.gov) before the real submission window opens.
  • Scholars draft a college application essay or, for career-pathway scholars, a documented cover letter and resume, using the same five-step BleuLearn Revision Protocol applied to every other documented essay in this curriculum.
  • Scholars complete a documented financial aid comparison worksheet for at least two real offers or projected net-price calculations, connecting directly to the Financial Architecture strand's budgeting skills.
  • Scholars who choose a non-college pathway complete an equivalent documented plan: apprenticeship application, military pathway research (if chosen), or a cooperative/small-business launch plan connecting to the Cooperative Economics strand.

⚠ Honest Note on What This Strand Still Needs

This scope and sequence is real and complete enough to teach from. What it does not yet have is a documented partnership list of specific HBCUs, apprenticeship programs, and employers willing to host visits or interviews — that requires Aurora's direct outreach and cannot be fabricated here as if it already existed. The free tools cited (O*NET, BLS, FAFSA, Virginia DOLI) are real, live, and verified as of this writing.
